FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

  • Question: How often should I water my plant? Answer: Overwatering is a common problem, so it's better to err on the side of underwatering. The frequency of watering depends on several factors, including the plant type, pot size, drainage, and environmental conditions. Generally, water when the top inch of soil feels dry to the touch, and let the excess water drain out of the pot bottom.

  • Question: Why are my plants leaves turning brown? Answer: Brown leaves can have several causes, such as overwatering, underwatering, lack of sunlight, nutrient deficiencies, pests, or diseases. Identifying the cause requires observing other signs and symptoms on the plant. Consult a gardening resource or professional for help if you're struggling to diagnose the problem.

  • Question: What are some easy plants to care for? Answer: Many plant options are suitable for beginners, even those with busy schedules. Some low-maintenance choices include: Snake Plant (Sansevieria trifasciata) ZZ Plant (Zamioculcas zamiifolia) Spider Plant (Chlorophytum comosum) Monstera Deliciosa Jade Plant (Crassula Ovata)

  • Question: How can I tell if my plant needs fertilizer? Answer: If your plant appears stunted, lacks vibrant color, or has slow growth, it might benefit from fertilizer. Most houseplants don't need frequent fertilization, usually only once a month or even less during their active growing season. Always follow the specific instructions on the fertilizer product and adjust the frequency depending on your plant's needs and environmental conditions.

  • Question: Can houseplants improve air quality in my home? Answer: Yes, certain houseplants can help remove some common indoor air pollutants like formaldehyde and benzene. However, research suggests their impact on overall air quality might be limited. Regardless, houseplants can still contribute to a more pleasant and natural environment within your home.
